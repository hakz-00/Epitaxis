Epistaxis Management Algorithm

A Comprehensive Guide to Nosebleed Treatment

⚠️ EMERGENCY SITUATIONS: Seek immediate medical attention if:
  • Bleeding continues for more than 20 minutes despite pressure
  • Large amount of blood loss causing dizziness, weakness, or fainting
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Bleeding following head injury
  • Taking blood thinners (anticoagulants)

Initial Assessment & First Aid

1
Patient Position
Sit the patient upright and lean slightly forward. This prevents blood from flowing down the throat and reduces the risk of aspiration.
2
Apply Direct Pressure
Pinch the soft part of the nose (just below the bony bridge) firmly for 10-15 minutes continuously. The patient should breathe through their mouth.
3
Cold Compress
Apply a cold compress or ice pack to the bridge of the nose and cheeks. This helps constrict blood vessels.
4
Reassess After 15 Minutes
After maintaining pressure, gently release and check if bleeding has stopped. If bleeding continues, reapply pressure for another 10-15 minutes.

Medical Treatment Options

1
Topical Vasoconstrictors
  • Oxymetazoline (Afrin) spray or soaked cotton pledgets
  • Phenylephrine solution
  • Cocaine solution (in clinical settings)
2
Cauterization
  • Silver nitrate sticks for visible bleeding points
  • Electrocautery for larger vessels
  • Apply after achieving hemostasis and with topical anesthetic
3
Nasal Packing
  • Absorbable packing (Gelfoam, Surgicel)
  • Non-absorbable packing (Merocel, petroleum gauze)
  • Balloon devices (Rapid Rhino, Epistat)
4
Posterior Packing
Required for posterior epistaxis:
  • Foley catheter technique
  • Commercial posterior packing devices
  • Requires hospital admission and monitoring
⚠️ IMPORTANT CONSIDERATIONS:
  • Document vital signs and estimate blood loss
  • Consider underlying causes (hypertension, anticoagulation, trauma)
  • Prophylactic antibiotics may be needed with prolonged packing
  • Follow-up with ENT specialist for recurrent epistaxis

Prevention & Aftercare

Preventive Measures
  • Use humidifier to maintain nasal moisture
  • Apply saline nasal spray regularly
  • Avoid nose picking and forceful blowing
  • Control hypertension if present
  • Review anticoagulation therapy with physician
Post-Treatment Instructions
  • Avoid strenuous activity for 24-48 hours
  • Sleep with head elevated
  • Avoid hot liquids and spicy foods
  • Do not remove nasal packing if present (follow medical advice)
  • Return if bleeding recurs or worsens
